Albert Einstein

"Imagination is more important than knowledge. For knowledge is limited, whereas imagination embraces the entire world, stimulating progress, giving birth to evolution."

Biography

Early Life
Birth: Born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany.
Education: Studied at the Polytechnic Institute in Zurich, where he graduated in 1900.

Career
Academia: Held positions at various universities including the University of Zurich, the University of Prague, and the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton.
Public Life: Einstein was also a prominent public figure, advocating for pacifism, civil rights, and Zionism. He moved to the United States in 1933 to escape the rise of the Nazi regime in Germany.

Legacy
Influence: Einstein's work laid the foundation for many modern technologies and scientific principles, influencing both theoretical and applied sciences.
Cultural Impact: Beyond science, Einstein became an icon of genius in popular culture, symbolizing intellectual achievement.

Einstein's theoretical advancements, along with his philosophical reflections on science and life, continue to inspire and shape contemporary thought.
